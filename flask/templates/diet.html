Diabetes Diet Guide

Nutritional recommendations for managing blood sugar levels

Foods to Include

Non-Starchy Vegetables

Broccoli, spinach, green beans, peppers, tomatoes, cauliflower

Lean Proteins

Chicken breast, fish, eggs, tofu, legumes, low-fat dairy

Whole Grains

Brown rice, quinoa, oatmeal, whole wheat bread, barley

Healthy Fats

Avocado, olive oil, nuts, seeds, fatty fish (salmon, mackerel)

Low-Glycemic Fruits

Berries, apples, pears, citrus fruits, cherries

Foods to Limit or Avoid

Sugary Foods

Candy, cookies, cake, ice cream, sweetened cereals

Refined Carbohydrates

White bread, white rice, regular pasta, pastries

Sugary Beverages

Soda, fruit juice, sweetened tea, energy drinks

Fried Foods

French fries, fried chicken, donuts, chips

Processed Foods

Packaged snacks, processed meats, fast food

Portion Control Tips
  • Use the plate method: 1/2 vegetables, 1/4 protein, 1/4 carbs.
  • Use smaller plates to control portions.
  • Measure portions until you can estimate accurately.
  • Eat slowly and stop when about 80% full.
  • Drink water before meals to reduce appetite.
Meal Timing Guidelines
  • Eat at regular times each day.
  • Don't skip meals, especially breakfast.
  • Space meals 4‚Äì5 hours apart when possible.
  • Have healthy snacks between meals if needed.
  • Avoid eating large meals late at night.
